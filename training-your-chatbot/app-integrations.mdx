Connect your chatbot to third-party services like Notion, OneNote, Google Drive, and Discord to use their content for training. This allows your chatbot to answer questions based on your existing documentation and knowledge bases.

## Available Integrations

From the **Apps** source type, you can connect:

- **Notion** - Connect your Notion workspace
- **Bookstack** - Connect your BookStack wiki ([detailed guide](/training-your-chatbot/bookstack))
- **OneNote** - Connect your Microsoft OneNote notebooks
- **Google Drive** - Connect your Google Drive documents
- **Discord** - Select Discord channels to train on

<Note>
  Intercom and Google Docs integrations are coming soon.
</Note>

## Connecting an App Integration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Training & Content">
    In your chatbot dashboard, click **Training & Content** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Sources">
    Click on **Sources**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Sources">
    Click the **+ Add Sources** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Apps">
    Select **Apps** from the source type dropdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your integration">
    Click on the integration you want to connect (e.g., Connect Notion, Connect OneNote).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize access">
    Follow the OAuth prompts to authorize SiteSpeakAI to access your content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Notion

Click **Connect Notion** to link your Notion workspace. You'll be redirected to Notion to log in and authorize access. Once connected, SiteSpeakAI can access your Notion pages to use for training.

  <img src="/images/training-your-chatbot/notion-oauth.png" alt="Notion login screen" />
</Frame>

## OneNote

Click **Connect OneNote** to link your Microsoft OneNote notebooks. You'll be prompted to sign in with your Microsoft account and grant permission.

SiteSpeakAI will be able to:
- View the titles of your OneNote notebooks and sections
- Read all pages in your notebooks

<Note>
  Password-protected sections cannot be accessed.
</Note>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/training-your-chatbot/onenote-oauth.png" alt="OneNote permission request" />
</Frame>

## Discord

To train your chatbot on Discord channel content, you first need to install the chatbot in your Discord server.

### Step 1: Install to Discord

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Install Agent">
    In your chatbot dashboard, click **Install Agent** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the Discord tab">
    Click on the **Discord** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect to Discord">
    Click the **Connect to Discord** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize access">
    Log in to Discord and authorize SiteSpeakAI to access your server.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/training-your-chatbot/discord-install.png" alt="Deploy & Install Discord tab" />
</Frame>

### Step 2: Select Channels for Training

Once Discord is connected, you can select which channels to train on:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Training & Content">
    Click **Training & Content â†’ Sources**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Sources">
    Click **+ Add Sources** and select **Apps**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Connect Discord Channels">
    Click **Connect Discord Channels**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select channels">
    Check the boxes next to the channels you want to train on (e.g., general, support, feature-requests).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to confirm your selection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you see "No channels found", make sure you have [installed the chatbot in your Discord server](#step-1-install-to-discord) first.
</Note>
